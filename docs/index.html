Yamete

Desk Impact Reactions for Mac

macOS 14+ · Apple Silicon

A macOS menu bar app that detects physical impacts on Apple Silicon MacBooks and responds with audio and visual feedback. Slap your desk, tap the laptop, get a reaction.

Features

Multi-Sensor Detection

Built-in accelerometer (Apple Silicon MacBooks), microphone, and AirPods IMU work together with configurable consensus.

Audio Reactions

Impact intensity selects from a library of sound clips. Harder hits play longer, louder sounds.

Flash Mode

Three-way visual response: off, full-screen overlay flash, or notification banner. Pick what fits your workflow.

Always-On Menu Bar

The menu bar face icon reacts on every detected impact, even with Flash Mode set to off.

40 Languages

Notification copy ships in 40 locales. The notification language can be selected independently of system language.

Fine-Tuned Control

Reactivity, volume, and flash opacity range sliders. Advanced detection gate tuning.

How It Works

Each sensor runs its own detection pipeline: raw data passes through bandpass filtering and a 6-gate analysis (spike threshold, rise rate, crest factor, confirmation count). When enough sensors agree within a time window, the app responds with sound and the selected visual response, scaled to impact intensity.

Yamete is a menu bar app (LSUIElement) — it has no Dock icon and no app windows. All controls live in the menu bar dropdown.

Privacy

Yamete makes zero network connections. All sensor data is processed locally in real-time. No audio is recorded or stored. Logs auto-delete after 24 hours. No accounts, no tracking, no ads.

Requirements

  • macOS 14.0+ (Sonoma), arm64 binary
  • Apple Silicon MacBook (M1/M2/M3/M4) for the built-in accelerometer
  • Any Mac with a microphone for sound-based impact detection
  • AirPods Pro / Beats for headphone motion sensing (optional)

Builds

Yamete ships in two flavors:

  • Yamete — Mac App Store build, rated 12+, ships with the tame notification copy.
  • Yamete Direct — notarized direct download, ships with the spicy notification copy. Not submitted to the App Store.

Both builds share the same detection engine, settings, and audio. They differ only in the notification text register.
